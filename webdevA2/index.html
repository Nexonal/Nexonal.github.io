What is a natural disaster?

By definition,

A natural disaster is a phenomenon caused by natural causes which results in a significant amount of damage to the environment or property as well as the injury of humans or the loss of people's lives. These events are not caused by humans but rather forces of nature

  • Earthquakes

    What is an earthquake by definition?

    An earthquake is the sudden shaking of the ground caused by the movement of rock beneath the Earth's surface. This movement typically happens along faults (cracks in the Earth's crust) when stress builds up and is suddenly released as seismic energy. Usually caused by the movement of the tectonic plate.

    Worst Earthquake:

    1556 Shaanxi Earthquake
    Location: Shaanxi Province, China



    Deaths: ~830,000
    Impact: Collapsed cave dwellings and entire villages; most fatal earthquake ever

    Tips on how to survive an Earthquake

    • Do not run out of your home if there is an Earthquake
    • If you are trapped, send a text or bang on a pipe or wall. Cover your mouth with your shirt for protection and instead of shouting, use a whistle
    • No matter where you are make sure to drop to the floor ,find cover example a sturdy or desk table and hid under it

  • Floods

    What is an flood by definition?

    A flood is the overflow of water onto normally dry land. It occurs when a body of water, like a river or stream, overflows its banks, or when surface water accumulates in areas that are typically dry. Flooding can be caused by various factors, including heavy rainfall, rapid snowmelt, storm surges, and dam or levee failures.

    Worst flooding:

    1931 China Floods
    Location: Yangtze, Huai, and Yellow Rivers, China



    Deaths: 1 to 4 million
    Impact: Deadliest natural disaster in recorded history

    Tips on how to survive an Flood

    • Unplug all electronics and make sure to get to high ground
    • Avoid camping or parking along streams, rivers, creeks, or other areas prone to flooding during heavy rainfall.
    • if trapped, do not panic. Remain calm and conserve your energy to the best of your abilities

  • Wildfire

    What is an Wildfire by definition?

    A wildfire is a fire that spreads uncontrolled through areas of natural vegetation like forests, grasslands, and brushlands. It can be caused by natural events like lightning or human actions like careless disposal of cigarettes. Wildfires can be incredibly destructive, impacting both the environment and human populations.

    Worst Wildfire:

    2003 Siberian Taiga Fires
    Location: Russia



    Area Burned: Over 47 million acres (about 190202Km^2)
    Impact: One of the largest forest fires in modern history

    Tips on how to survive an wildfire

    • Most importantly protect your lungs by breathing air closest to the ground or through a mask or wet cloth to reduce inhalation of smoke
    • Don’t try to outrun the fire but look for a body of water and crouch in it
    • Know your evacuation route ahead of time and prepare an evacuation checklist and supplies in case of a wildfire

  • Tornados

    What is an Tornado by definition?

    A tornado by definition is a violent rotating column of air extending from a thunderstorm to the ground often visible as a funnel-shaped cloud. Usually caused by a specific combination of weather conditions that create powerful, rotating storms.

    Worst Tornado:

    Tri-State Tornado 1925
    Location: United States – Missouri, Illinois, and Indiana



    Deaths : 695 Injured : 2,000 over
    Impact: longest tornado path ever recorded (352km)

    Tips on how to survive an Tornado

    • One basic rule is AVOID WINDOWS. An exploding window can injure or kill.
    • hide in the This could be a center hallway, bathroom, or closet for protection
    • Whereever you go, know where the closest shelter is from you

  • Landslides

    What is an Landdslide by definition?

    A landslide is defined as the movement of a mass of rock, debris, or earth down a slope. Landslides are a type of "mass wasting," which denotes any down-slope movement of soil and rock under the direct influence of gravity.

    Worst Landslide:

    1970 Huascarán Landslide (Avalanche-triggered by Earthquake)
    Location: Yungay, Peru



    Deaths : about 20,000
    Impact: Entire town wiped out in seconds

    Tips on how to survive an Landslides

    • Look out for signs of landslides like tilted or bent trees, Erosion, Movement of soil away from foundation
    • Your best choice is to evacuate before the landslide happens
    • Monitor the weather, if it rains do be prepared in case of a landslide, avoid river valleys and low lying areas during the danger
