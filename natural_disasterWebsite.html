What is a natural disaster?

By definition,

A natural disaster is a phenomenon caused by natural causes which results in a significant amount of damage to the environment or property as well as the injury of humans or the loss of people's lives. These events are not caused by humans but rather forces of nature

  • Earthquakes

    What is an earthquake by definition?

    An earthquake is the sudden shaking of the ground caused by the movement of rock beneath the Earth's surface. This movement typically happens along faults (cracks in the Earth's crust) when stress builds up and is suddenly released as seismic energy. Usually caused by the movement of the tectonic plate.

    Worst Earthquake:

    1556 Shaanxi Earthquake
    Location: Shaanxi Province, China



    Deaths: ~830,000
    Impact: Collapsed cave dwellings and entire villages; most fatal earthquake ever

    Tips on how to survive an Earthquake

    • Do not run out of your home if there is an Earthquake

    • If you are trapped, send a text or bang on a pipe or wall. Cover your mouth with your shirt for protection and instead of shouting, use a whistle

    • No matter where you are make sure to drop to the floor ,find cover example a sturdy or desk table and hid under it

  • Floods

    What is an flood by definition?

    A flood is the overflow of water onto normally dry land. It occurs when a body of water, like a river or stream, overflows its banks, or when surface water accumulates in areas that are typically dry. Flooding can be caused by various factors, including heavy rainfall, rapid snowmelt, storm surges, and dam or levee failures.

    Worst flooding:

    Location: Yangtze, Huai, and Yellow Rivers, China

    Impact: Deadliest natural disaster in recorded history
